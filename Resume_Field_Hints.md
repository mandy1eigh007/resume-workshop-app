# Resume Field Hints

**Purpose:** Guidance and hints for filling out each resume field with construction-focused examples.

**Audience:** Students and instructors using the resume builder app.

---

## Header Section

### Name
**What to include:**
* Full legal name (first and last)
* Middle initial optional

**Example:**
* John A. Smith
* Maria Rodriguez

**Avoid:**
* Nicknames (unless you go by that name professionally)
* Titles (Mr., Ms., etc.)

---

### City, State
**What to include:**
* City where you currently reside
* State abbreviation

**Example:**
* Seattle, WA
* Tacoma, WA
* Everett, WA

**Note:**
* No street address needed for privacy
* Use work authorization location if different from residence

---

### Phone
**What to include:**
* Mobile number preferred
* 10-digit format

**Example:**
* (206) 555-1234
* 253-555-5678

**Best practices:**
* Use a number where you can be reached during business hours
* Set up professional voicemail

---

### Email
**What to include:**
* Professional email address
* Clear, simple format

**Example:**
* john.smith@email.com
* maria.r.rodriguez@email.com

**Avoid:**
* Unprofessional addresses (partyguy99@, etc.)
* Overly complex addresses

**Tip:**
* Create a new email for job searching if needed

---

## Objective Section

### Objective Statement
**What to include:**
* One-line statement of your goal
* Neutral, evidence-ready language
* Focus on what you want to learn or contribute

**Examples:**
* "Seeking structured training to assemble, fit, and torque pressure-rated components to spec; day-one focus on safety, rigging signals, and QA logs."
* "Learn layout, framing, and fastening to prints; log measurements, fastener counts, and inspections."

**How to select:**
1. Choose your target trade from dropdown
2. Select apprenticeship or job category
3. Pick an objective from the starter bank
4. Edit if needed to match your specific goals

**Avoid:**
* Generic statements ("Looking for a challenging opportunity")
* Adjective-heavy phrases ("Hard-working, motivated individual")
* Sub-trade labels (e.g., "06 Electrician" instead of "Electrician – Limited Energy")
* Union/non-union references

**Length:**
* 1–2 lines maximum

## Experience Section

### Company Name
**What to include:**
* Employer's business name
* Keep it simple and clear

**Example:**
* ABC Restaurant
* Metro Transit
* Seattle Retail Co.

**Avoid:**
* Corporate legal names unless necessary (e.g., "ABC Restaurant LLC")
* Abbreviations that aren't commonly known

---

### City, State
**What to include:**
* City and state where you worked
* State abbreviation

**Example:**
* Seattle, WA
* Portland, OR

---

### Job Title
**What to include:**
* Your actual job title
* Use standardized role names from detection index if possible

**Example:**
* Line Cook
* Warehouse Associate
* Material Handler
* Carpenter Helper

**Avoid:**
* Made-up titles
* Inflated titles (e.g., "Senior Expert" when you were entry-level)

---

### Start Date – End Date
**What to include:**
* Month and year format (MM/YYYY)
* "Present" if currently employed

**Example:**
* 06/2022 – 12/2024
* 01/2023 – Present

**Tips:**
* Be accurate—employers verify dates
* Gaps are okay; focus on what you did accomplish

---

### Bullets (Duty Descriptions)
**What to include:**
* Measured, evidence-ready descriptions
* Quantities, tools, or outcomes
* 3–4 bullets per job
* ≤24 words each

**Structure:**
* [Action verb] + [What you did] + [How/With what] + [Measurement/Outcome]

**Examples:**
* "Prepped, portioned, and labeled 60–90 covers/shift; maintained FIFO logs and line temps."
* "Picked 80–200 lines/shift; recorded pick rates."
* "Staged lumber/hardware; counts and labels."

**Avoid:**
* Adjective-only bullets ("Hard worker; team player")
* Vague statements ("Helped with various tasks")
* Overly long bullets (>24 words)

**Tips:**
* Use the bullet bank suggestions for your detected role
* Edit to match your specific experience
* Ask: Can I attach proof of this? (If not, make it more specific)

## Skills Section

### Skill Selection
**What to include:**
* Skills organized into 3 categories:
  * Transferable (problem solving, time management, etc.)
  * Job-Specific (reading blueprints, hand tools, etc.)
  * Self-Management (initiative, willingness to learn, etc.)
* ≤12 total skills

**How to select:**
1. Browse skills by category
2. Select skills you can demonstrate
3. Stop at 12 total

**Examples:**
* Transferable: Attention to Detail, Team Collaboration, Safety Mindset
* Job-Specific: Hand & Power Tools, Measuring & Layout, Materials Handling
* Self-Management: Initiative, Willingness to Learn, Physical Stamina

**Avoid:**
* Listing more than 12 skills (quality over quantity)
* Skills you can't demonstrate or discuss in an interview
* Overly generic skills ("Computer skills" without specifics)

**Tips:**
* Choose skills that align with your target trade
* Mix categories to show well-rounded capabilities

## Certifications Section

### Certification Entry
**What to include:**
* Normalized certification name
* Use the certification normalization table

**Examples:**
* OSHA Outreach 10-Hour (Construction)
* WA Flagger (expires three years from issuance)
* CPR/First Aid
* EPA Section 608 (if earned)

**How to enter:**
1. Type the certification name
2. App will suggest normalized name
3. Select the correct match

**Avoid:**
* Listing certifications you don't have
* Including expiration dates on the resume (track separately)
* Using variant names (e.g., "OSHA-10" instead of "OSHA Outreach 10-Hour (Construction)")

**Note on forklift:**
* List as "Forklift — employer evaluation on hire" if you have training
* Employers will evaluate competency on hire

## General Best Practices

### Do:
* ✓ Be honest and accurate
* ✓ Use measured, specific language
* ✓ Focus on what you can prove
* ✓ Keep it to one page
* ✓ Proofread for spelling and grammar

### Don't:
* ✗ Invent employers, dates, or skills
* ✗ Use adjectives without evidence
* ✗ Include photos or graphics
* ✗ List references on resume
* ✗ Use first-person pronouns (I, me, my)

### Tone:
* Neutral, factual, crew-useful language
* No fluff or exaggeration
* Evidence-ready (every bullet should have a story or proof)
