# Resume Field Hints

> Field-level hints for resume fields

## Header Fields

### Name
* Use full legal name or preferred professional name
* Format: First Last or First Middle Last

### City, State
* Use current city and state abbreviation
* Format: Seattle, WA
* If relocating, use target location

### Phone
* Use reliable contact number
* Format: 206-555-1234
* Ensure voicemail is professional

### Email
* Use professional email address
* Avoid nicknames or unprofessional handles
* Check regularly for responses

---

## Objective

### Guidelines
* Keep to one line (≤200 characters)
* Select from provided starters or edit carefully
* Maintain neutral, factual tone
* Focus on evidence and readiness
* **Avoid:** union/non-union references, sub-trade labels

### Examples (Good)
* "Seeking structured training to assemble, fit, and torque pressure-rated components to spec; day-one focus on safety, rigging signals, and QA logs."
* "Can stage parts, read basic marks, and torque fasteners to spec with recorded readings; strong housekeeping."

### Examples (Avoid)
* "Hard worker seeking any construction job" (too vague)
* "Looking for union electrician apprenticeship" (union reference)
* "Experienced residential framer seeking position" (sub-trade label)

---

## Experience

### Company
* Use official company name
* Be accurate and verifiable

### City/State
* Location of work performed
* Format: City, ST

### Dates
* Format: MM/YYYY – MM/YYYY or MM/YYYY – Present
* Be accurate and honest
* **No gaps that can't be explained**

### Title
* Use your actual job title
* If informal, use closest standard equivalent

### Bullets (3–4 per job)

**Guidelines:**
* ≤24 words per bullet
* Include measurements and counts
* Use past tense for previous jobs
* Focus on evidence of safety, pace, tool control, documentation

**Structure:**
* Action + measurable detail + outcome/proof
* Example: "Staged lumber/hardware; counts and labels." (6 words)
* Example: "Picked 80–200 lines/shift; recorded pick rates." (7 words)

**What to include:**
* Quantities (counts, weights, distances, areas)
* Frequencies (per shift, per day, weekly)
* Tools/equipment used
* Documentation kept
* Safety practices followed

**What to avoid:**
* Adjectives without evidence ("hard worker," "great team player")
* Vague duties ("helped with various tasks")
* Responsibilities without proof ("responsible for safety")
* First person ("I did...", "My role was...")

## Skills (≤12 total across all three categories)

### Transferable Skills
Focus on soft skills that apply across roles:
* Problem Solving
* Time Management
* Communication Under Pressure
* Documentation & Recordkeeping

### Job-Specific Skills
Focus on construction/trade-specific abilities:
* Reading Blueprints & Specs
* Hand & Power Tools
* Measuring & Layout
* Rigging Signals (awareness/practice)

### Self-Management Skills
Focus on work habits and personal conduct:
* Initiative
* Willingness to Learn
* Physical Stamina
* Following Directions

**Guidelines:**
* Select ≤12 total skills
* Choose skills you can demonstrate or prove
* Balance across all three categories
* Skills should align with your bullets and objectives

---

## Certifications

**Enter certifications exactly as earned.**

The app will normalize to standard phrasing:
* OSHA 10 → OSHA Outreach 10-Hour (Construction)
* OSHA 30 → OSHA Outreach 30-Hour (Construction)
* WA Flagger → WA Flagger (expires three years from issuance)
* Forklift → Forklift — employer evaluation on hire
* CPR/First Aid → CPR/First Aid
* EPA 608 → EPA Section 608 (if earned)

**Important notes:**
* Keep original cards/certificates accessible
* Track expiry dates for time-limited credentials
* Forklift: employer evaluates on hire; pre-hire classes are practice only

## Education (≤2 entries)

### School
* Official school name
* High school, GCC, technical college, etc.

### Credential
* Diploma, GED, Certificate, Associate, etc.
* Be specific if specialized (e.g., "Certificate in Welding")

### Year
* Year of completion or expected completion
* Format: YYYY

### Details (optional)
* GPA if strong (≥3.5)
* Honors or awards
* Relevant coursework
* Keep brief (≤50 characters)

---

## General Constraints

**One-page resume:**
* All content must fit on one page
* Use concise, measured language
* Prioritize most relevant experience
* Cut older/unrelated experience if needed

**Tone:**
* Neutral and factual
* Evidence-driven
* Crew-useful language
* No hype or exaggeration

**Red Flags to Avoid:**
* Invented employers or dates
* Adjective-only bullets
* Sub-trade labels in objectives
* Union/non-union wording anywhere
* Vague or unmeasurable claims
